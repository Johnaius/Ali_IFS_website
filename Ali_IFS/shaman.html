Shamanic Practitioner

"A shamanic practitioner can help someone by using techniques and practices to restore balance and promote healing in the individual's physical, emotional, and spiritual well-being. This may involve energy healing, divination, and spiritual guidance, and is intended to complement, but not replace, conventional medical treatment."

Shamanic practice is a spiritual tradition that has roots in many cultures, including indigenous cultures in North and South America, Asia, Africa, Ireland and Australia. It is based on the belief that everything in the universe is interconnected and that there is a spiritual aspect to all things, including plants, animals, and the natural world.


In shamanic practice, the shamanic practitioner, or shaman, acts as a mediator between the physical and spiritual worlds as well as a guide, using various techniques to bring balance and healing to individuals and communities. Some of the key techniques used in shamanic practice include:


  • Vision quest: A vision quest is a solitary spiritual journey, typically taken in nature, during which the shaman seeks guidance and insights from the spirit world.
  • Soul retrieval: Soul retrieval is a technique used to help individuals who have experienced trauma or loss to heal and reintegrate parts of their soul that may have become fragmented as a result of the traumatic experience.
  • Extraction: Extraction is a technique used to remove negative or harmful energies from the individual's energy field that may be causing physical or emotional distress.
  • Power animal retrieval: A power animal retrieval is a technique used to identify an individual's spirit animal, which is believed to offer guidance, protection, and support to the individual.
  • Drumming and chanting: Drumming and chanting are used to enter into an altered state of consciousness, known as shamanic trance, in which the shaman is able to communicate with the spirit world.
  • Illumination Process: Illumination is a shamanic technique used to identify and release limiting beliefs and patterns in the individual's energy field, promoting healing, personal growth, and spiritual transformation.
  • Feeding your demons: a shamanic practice that involves confronting and embracing one's fears and negative emotions in order to transform them into sources of strength and wisdom, promoting emotional healing and spiritual growth.

Shamanic practice has been used for centuries to address physical, emotional, and spiritual issues, and is increasingly being recognized as a complementary form of healing. However, as with any form of complementary or alternative medicine, it's important to approach shamanic practice with caution and to choose a qualified and experienced practitioner
