Substance Abuse

As someone who specializes in IFS, Shamanic Practices, and Usui Reiki, my goal is to help clients overcome addiction and develop a greater sense of self-awareness, self-compassion, and purpose. I believe that addiction is a complex and multifaceted issue that requires a holistic and integrative approach to healing. By drawing on a variety of modalities and techniques, I can help clients develop the skills and tools they need to achieve lasting recovery and wellness.

-Alison Lesakowski

Substance abuse counseling is a specialized form of therapy that is designed to help individuals overcome addiction and dependence on drugs or alcohol. Substance abuse counselors work with clients to identify the root causes of their addiction, and develop personalized treatment plans to help them achieve lasting recovery and wellness.


One of the key goals of substance abuse counseling is to help clients develop a greater sense of self-awareness and self-compassion. By learning to understand and accept themselves, clients can begin to heal from the emotional and psychological wounds that may have contributed to their addiction. Substance abuse counselors often use a variety of techniques, such as cognitive-behavioral therapy, motivational interviewing, and family therapy, to help clients develop greater self-awareness and insight.


Another important aspect of substance abuse counseling is education. Counselors work with clients to help them understand the nature of addiction, the effects of drugs and alcohol on the body and mind, and the various treatment options that are available. Education can help clients make informed decisions about their recovery, and can also help them develop the skills and knowledge they need to maintain their sobriety over the long-term.


Substance abuse counseling is a collaborative process, with the counselor and client working together as a team to achieve the client's goals. The counselor provides support, guidance, and encouragement, while the client takes an active role in their own recovery. By working together, the counselor and client can develop a personalized treatment plan that takes into account the client's unique needs, strengths, and challenges, and that is tailored to their individual goals and aspirations.
